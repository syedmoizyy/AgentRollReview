# Interview Guide

## Moderator setup

Explain that this is research on launch-review decisions, not a product sales session. Ask permission before recording. Do not disclose confidential model, customer, or incident details. The fictional prototype data are sample evidence, not measured results.

## Opening (5 minutes)

- What is your role in launching AI-enabled or tool-using workflows?
- Tell me about the most recent launch review you participated in.
- What decision did you personally own, recommend, or influence?

## Recent-decision walkthrough (20 minutes)

- What triggered the review, and what was at stake?
- Who participated? Who could approve, delay, or block?
- What evidence did you inspect, and where did it live?
- Which evidence did you trust least? Why?
- Were thresholds agreed before results were visible?
- Describe a failure that changed—or should have changed—the decision.
- How did you account for low-frequency but severe consequences?
- Where did reviewers disagree? How was that disagreement recorded and resolved?
- What made the final decision defensible or difficult to defend later?
- What happened to unresolved risks and follow-up actions?

## Outcome language (10 minutes)

Present Ship, Investigate, and Block without examples first.

- How would you distinguish Investigate from Block?
- What must always prevent Ship?
- What missing evidence is tolerable, and what is not?
- Who, if anyone, may override a blocking recommendation? What record is required?
- Does any term create ambiguity in your organization?

## Concept walkthrough (15 minutes)

Show the fictional scenario and ask the participant to think aloud.

- What would you inspect first?
- What do you believe the recommendation is, and why?
- Can you trace each reason to evidence or policy?
- Is reviewer disagreement sufficiently visible?
- What would you need before signing your role's assessment?
- What is unnecessary or burdensome?
- What is missing that would prevent a real decision?
- Where might this create false confidence?

Do not ask whether they “like” the product until behavioral questions are exhausted.

## Closing (5 minutes)

- Who else should be involved in this research?
- What artifact from a real review could you safely describe or redact?
- What did we fail to ask about launch decisions?

## Role-specific probes

- **Product:** user-value tradeoffs, decision ownership, deadlines, residual-risk acceptance.
- **Engineering:** reproducibility, denominators, dependency failures, recovery, evidence versioning.
- **Trust/safety:** consequence severity, affected segments, scenario coverage, veto/override.
- **Operations:** detection, escalation, rollback, staffing assumptions, post-launch conditions.

## Avoid leading questions

Avoid “Would one dashboard help?” and “Is auditability important?” Prefer “Show me how you assembled the decision” and “What happened when someone questioned it later?”
